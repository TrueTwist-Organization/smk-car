Track / Tech

POWERTRAIN

At the heart of the Bugatti Bolide lies an extraordinary 8.0-liter W16 engine, equipped with four high-performance turbochargers. This engineering masterpiece produces an astonishing 1,850 PS and 1,850 Nm of torque, making it one of the most powerful internal combustion engines ever created for a track-focused hypercar.

To achieve this exceptional level of performance, Bugatti engineers have extensively optimized the engine for higher rotational speeds and maximum efficiency. The intake and exhaust systems have been completely re-engineered to improve airflow, reduce resistance, and enhance combustion efficiency at extreme performance levels.

The four newly developed turbochargers feature advanced, aerodynamically optimized turbine blades. These enhancements allow the system to generate higher boost pressure, especially at elevated engine speeds, ensuring a continuous surge of power and rapid throttle response under demanding track conditions.

A major advancement can also be found in the cooling system. The traditional air-to-air intercooling system with water pre-cooling has been replaced by a more advanced dual water-to-air cooling setup. This system ensures significantly more stable and consistent intake air temperatures, even during prolonged high-speed driving on the track.

As a result, the W16 engine is capable of maintaining peak performance for extended periods without thermal degradation. This level of thermal stability is crucial for a track-only machine like the Bolide, where sustained power delivery and reliability are just as important as outright speed.

The Bugatti Bolide is not merely powered by an engine — it is driven by a masterpiece of engineering precision, designed to push the absolute limits of internal combustion performance.
